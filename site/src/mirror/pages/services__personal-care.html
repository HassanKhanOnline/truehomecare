Our Specialist Personal Care Support

Hygiene and Bathing Assistance

Our caregivers provide gentle, dignified support with bathing and showering. We prioritise skin integrity and hygiene to maintain physical comfort, helping clients feel refreshed, secure, and well-cared for.

Dressing and Grooming Support

We offer respectful assistance with daily clothing choices and personal grooming. These vital tasks help our clients maintain their self-esteem, independence, and a sense of identity.

Mobility and Transfer Help

We ensure safe movement around the home through professional positioning and transfer assistance. Our team reduces fall risks and provides specialist support for Parkinson’s clients to improve confidence and mobility.

Continence and Toileting Care

We provide discreet and dignified continence support. Our approach focuses on privacy and respect, ensuring the client feels secure, comfortable, and well-supported during all personal assistance.

Medication Reminders

To reduce the risk of missed dosages, we provide timely prompts for medication adherence. We liaise with GPs, pharmacists, and district nurses to ensure health stability and peace of mind.

Nutrition and Meal Support

Our team provides nutritional support tailored to individual health requirements, ensuring balanced diets that promote wellbeing. For specific dietary guidance, we refer to resources provided by Diabetes UK.

Our Approach to Person-Centred Care

The Step-by-Step Care Planning Process

We ensure a seamless transition to home care through a structured onboarding process:

  • Initial Consultation: A detailed needs analysis to understand the client’s preferences, health conditions, and personal requirements. We also discuss how we can provide respite and emotional support for family members.
  • Risk Assessment: A comprehensive evaluation of the home environment to ensure safety, mitigate hazards, and plan for any specific needs, such as Parkinson’s mobility support or palliative care requirements.
  • Bespoke Care Plan: The creation of a personalised strategy tailored to the client’s specific daily routines—including support forwaking nights, sleeping nights, or outings such as walks in the park, movies, or hospital visits.

Carer Qualifications and Vetting

Our team comprises skilled professionals, care managers, care coordinators, and caregivers who are experts in palliative home care. To maintain clinical excellence, our staff undergo rigorous screening:

  • Strict Vetting: All staff pass comprehensive DBS checks and professional background screening.
  • Training Standards: Ongoing professional training to ensure caregivers remain current with best practices, including specialist support for Alzheimer and Dementia Care.
  • Quality Monitoring: Continuous oversight to maintain high standards of clinical delivery, strictly adhering to the Ethical Framework by Stockport Council.

Integration of Assistive Technology

We use smart tools to enhance safety and independence, employing monitoring technology that improves emergency response times and provides real-time peace of mind for both the client and their family.

Family Collaboration and Communication

We maintain transparent communication channels and conduct regular care reviews with family members to ensure the support evolves alongside the client’s needs. We work alongside other healthcare professionals like district nurses, GPs, hospitals, and pharmacists to ensure integrated, high-quality care.
