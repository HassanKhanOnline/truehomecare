13 Best Snacks for Seniors with Diabetes: Sweet & Savory Ideas

Saima Adil Zafar

15 January, 2025

If you or someone you love has diabetes, you or they must be really careful with their diet, especially when it comes to choosing snacks. For seniors, it becomes absolutely necessary to maintain their blood sugar levels, for which they need to carefully choose what they eat. 

Now, having diabetes does not mean that you can’t have snacks in your diet. Nutritious snacks can be just what your body needs to stabilize your blood sugar levels, lift your mood, and give you the energy you need to perform daily tasks. 

In this article, we will explore 13 of the best snacks for seniors with diabetes, which are packed with both sweet and savory nutrition.

30 Seconds Summary

  • Diabetes is a medical condition that occurs when your body is either unable to produce insulin, i.e., type 1 diabetes, or is not able to effectively use the insulin it produces, i.e., type 2 diabetes.
  • When choosing snacks for diabetics, look for ones that are low in sugar and carbohydrates and have a high protein and fiber content. 
  • Some delicious sweet snacks for diabetics include Peanut Butter Energy Balls, Greek Yogurt Bark with Berries, Prunes, Avocados, Chia Pudding with Almond Milk, Berries, and Apple Slices with Nut Butter.
  • Some delicious savory snacks for diabetics include Roasted Chickpeas, Cucumber Sandwiches, Hard-Boiled Eggs with Avocado, Turkey Roll-Ups, Savory Greek Yogurt Dip, and Masala Omelet. 

What is Diabetes?

Before we look into the snacks for diabetics, let’s first understand what diabetes is.

So, diabetes is a medical condition that occurs when your body is either unable to produce insulin, i.e., type 1 diabetes, or is not able to effectively use the insulin it produces, i.e., type 2 diabetes. Now, what is Insulin? Insulin is a hormone that helps absorb sugar in the bloodstream to be used for energy. The absence of insulin means that your blood sugar levels will rise to dangerous levels and could lead to life-threatening situations such as heart disease or even stroke.

Factors To Consider When Choosing Diabetic-Friendly Snacks

When you are exploring diabetic-friendly snack ideas, it is important to consider a few factors, including:

  1. Choose snacks that have a low sugar content to avoid blood sugar spikes.
  2. Choose snacks with high fiber and protein content for satiety and steady energy.
  3. Choose snacks with appropriate nutrient density; this will support your overall health.
  4. Choose easy preparation or grab-and-go options for convenience.

Sweet Snacks for Diabetes

  1. Peanut Butter Energy Balls

These simple and tiny peanut butter balls are a quick snack for seniors with diabetes. They contain a mix of simple and complex carbohydrates that will help you when you feel low on energy. No-bake treats can be made using ingredients already available in your home, such as oats and peanut butter. You can even add your choice of dried fruits and chopped nuts. 

  1. Greek Yogurt Bark with Berries

Did you know that yogurt is low in carbs and high in proteins, especially if you choose a plain or lightly flavored yogurt? For a balanced snack for seniors with diabetes, you can mix your Greek yogurt with fresh berries and sprinkle a few nuts for a refreshing yet protein-packed snack that is sure to satisfy your cravings. 

  1. Prunes

Prunes are a healthy snack for seniors with diabetes. They are chewy, sweet, and tasty. These tiny dried fruits are packed with nutrition and have plenty of health benefits, including gastrointestinal and bone health. 

Moreover, prunes are high in fiber; a serving of four prunes only contains three grams of fiber. You can either have them alone or mix them with some yogurt for a high-fiber and high-protein diet. 

  1. Avocados 

Did you know that avocados are also rich in fiber? The high fiber content of monosaturated fatty acids found in avocados can help regulate your blood sugar levels. Moreover, there are only 8 grams of carbs in one hundred grams of avocados, making them one of the low-sugar snacks for seniors with diabetes. 

One study conducted in 2023 found that consuming avocados is associated with lower levels of HbA1c and fasting blood sugar. 

  1. Chia Pudding with Almond Milk

Another healthy snack for seniors with diabetes is chia seed pudding. To make it, soak chia seeds in unsweetened milk overnight. You can even add a touch of vanilla and top it with a few fresh fruits for a fiber-rich treat. 

Did you know that chia seeds are rich in many nutrients, including proteins and fiber, that help stabilize blood sugar levels? 

  1. Berries

Did you know that berries are high in fiber, and raspberries have the highest fiber content, i.e., a cup of raspberries contains 9 grams of fiber? Moreover, berries are also rich in antioxidants that can help fight cell damage and inflammation. 

For diabetes patients, the fiber in the berries slows down digestion, regulating the sugar release in the bloodstream. You can even pair berries with a protein, such as yogurt, for an even more balanced diet. 

  1. Apple Slices with Nut Butter

Pairing apple slices with nut butter, such as peanut butter, delivers the right amount of fiber, proteins, and vitamins. Crisp apple slices with almond or peanut butter make a naturally sweet and nutritious snack.

Savory Snacks for Diabetes

  1. Roasted Chickpeas

One of the best snacks for managing blood sugar is roasted chickpeas. Chickpeas are packed with fiber and protein, and when roasted with spices like paprika or cumin, they make delicious yet fulfilling snacks with plenty of anti-inflammatory benefits. Not only is this a high-protein and high-fiber snack, but it also travels well, keeping your blood sugar levels in check. 

  1. Cucumber Sandwiches

English cucumber sandwiches are a great healthy snack idea for individuals with diabetes. To make them, just layer a few slices of cucumber over a whole grain bread. They are both low-carb and high-protein options. 

  1. Hard-Boiled Eggs with Avocado

Eggs are packed with proteins, while avocados are packed with fibers. By pairing both of them together, you can create a meal that is both tasty and healthy. You have to slice a boiled egg in half, top it with mashed avocado, and sprinkle some pepper. 

  1. Turkey Roll-Ups

Turkey roll-ups are another healthy snack for seniors with diabetes that is low in carbs and high in proteins. The proteins in turkey roll-ups help lower one’s appetite. To make a turkey roll-up, you simply need to spread a spoonful of cream cheese on a turkey slice and add some sliced non-starch veggies such as cucumbers and bell peppers. 

  1. Savory Greek Yogurt Dip

As mentioned earlier, yogurt is high in protein, which helps stabilize your blood sugar levels. If you don’t like your yogurt sweet, you can also make a savory Greek yogurt dip. Simply mix plain Greek yogurt with herbs like dill, garlic, and parsley. You can also add raw vegetables for a nutrient-dense snack.

  1. Masala Omelet

Although hard-boiled eggs are a great source of protein, you might not always want to have them boiled. Add a variety to your meals by making an omelet with chopped onions, tomatoes, spinach, and spices. This is not only a protein-rich snack but will also help you feel fuller for longer. 

Tips for Choosing the Best Snacks for Seniors with Diabetes

So, how can you choose the best snacks for seniors? Here are a few tips:

  • Read Labels: Look for snacks with no added sugar and natural ingredients.
  • Portion Control: Keep the portions small to avoid overeating.
  • Prepare Ahead: You can always prepare snacks such as energy balls and roasted chickpeas in snacks, so you can have them when you are craving something. 
  • Pair Protein, Fibers, and Carbs: Prefer snacks that combine proteins, fibers, and some carbs for a balanced meal. 

Over to You

With these best sweet and savory snacks for diabetics, seniors can enjoy delicious treats while keeping themselves healthy.
